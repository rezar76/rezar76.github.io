Reza Ramezanpour

PhD researcher in wireless communications & graph machine learning

rr68 ·at· rice.edu

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Rice University, advised by Prof. Ashutosh Sabharwal. I also work closely with Prof. Santiago Segarra on graph machine learning. I am currently a Visiting Graduate Student at the University of California, San Diego.

My research sits at the intersection of wireless communications and machine learning. On the wireless side, I work on resource allocation, integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), and next-generation (NextG) systems. On the learning side, I study graph machine learning — graphon learning, graph neural networks, and unsupervised representation learning — together with the statistical learning theory, convex optimization, and reinforcement learning that underpin them.

Before Rice, I earned my B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from Sharif University of Technology in Tehran, Iran.
